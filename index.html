About this Project

SAMS began as a coursework project for our Web Programming module, but quickly grew into something we actually wanted to use. Most attendance tools we tried were either heavy enterprise systems or one-off spreadsheets that nobody updated past week three. We aimed for something in between: simple enough to actually use every class, structured enough to trust at exam time.

This first release focuses on the front-end — the structure, layout and visual design of the system. The pages you see across the site are static, but they reflect the real screens a future version of SAMS would have: a dashboard for instructors, a student list with attendance marking, and a contact channel for support.

"An attendance system should disappear into the background of a class — not become another thing the instructor has to manage."

What the system covers

  1. Daily attendance marking for individual courses.
  2. Per-student attendance history and trends.
  3. Configurable warning thresholds (e.g. below 75%).
  4. Printable course-level summary reports.
